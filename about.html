FAQs

Solar energy offers numerous benefits, including reduced electricity bills, lower carbon footprints, energy independence, and minimal maintenance costs. It is a clean, renewable energy source that contributes to environmental sustainability.

A solar water heater uses solar collectors to absorb sunlight and heat a liquid (usually water) in a storage tank. This system provides an eco-friendly way to heat water without relying on electricity or gas, saving both money and energy.

A solar on-grid system is connected to the local utility grid. It allows you to generate electricity from the sun while still being able to draw power from the grid when needed. If your solar system generates more power than you consume, the excess energy can be sent back to the grid, often earning you credits.

Savings depend on factors such as the size of your system, energy consumption, and local energy rates. However, solar energy typically leads to significant reductions in electricity bills, with most systems paying for themselves within 5-7 years.
